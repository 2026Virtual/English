Question

Some people think that most crimes are the result of circumstances like poverty and other social problems. 


Others believe that they are caused by people who are bad by nature.


Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

The debate on why people commit crimes usually falls into two camps: one side believes that tough situations like poverty and poor education lead people to crime, while the other thinks some people are just naturally prone to breaking the law.


Those who think environment plays a big role argue that if you're living in poverty, struggling to get by, or growing up in a rough area, you might turn to crime as a way to cope or survive. High crime rates in areas with lots of unemployment support this idea. Basically, when people feel backed into a corner, they might steal or vandalize just to make ends meet.


On the flip side, some say that crime comes down to a person's nature. They believe that some people are just wired differently, which makes them more likely to commit crimes. This could be because of their genetics, their personality, or even issues with how their brain works. Even people from wealthy backgrounds can end up committing crimes like fraud or drug-related offenses, which suggests that bad circumstances aren't always to blame.


Personally, I lean towards the idea that where and how we live plays a bigger role in leading someone to crime than their natural tendencies. There's good evidence that improving environmental factors like education and job opportunities can really help lower crime rates. This approach is not just about reducing crime, but also about helping people and making society better overall.


In conclusion, while it’s important to consider that some people might have a natural inclination towards crime, a lot of evidence points to external circumstances as a major factor. Addressing these can help prevent crime and improve lives, making it a win-win for everyone.

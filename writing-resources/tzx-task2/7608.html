Question

Some people think rapid population growth in cities only benefits people who live there while others believe that it has negative impacts.


Discuss both these views and give your own opinion.


Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

The rapid growth of urban populations is a topic of considerable debate. While some argue that it primarily benefits city dwellers, others believe it brings negative consequences. This essay will examine both perspectives and provide a personal opinion.


On one hand, proponents of rapid population growth in cities argue that it offers significant benefits. As cities expand, more people gain access to better job opportunities, education, and healthcare. Urbanization often leads to economic development, as businesses grow and new industries emerge, creating a wider range of employment options. Additionally, cities tend to have better infrastructure, including transportation and utilities, which improve the quality of life for their residents. For example, in cities like Melbourne and New York, rapid population growth has spurred innovations in technology and services that directly benefit those living in these urban areas.


However, others believe that the rapid growth of cities brings about negative impacts, both for the residents and the environment. First, the increased population leads to overcrowding, resulting in higher living costs, limited affordable housing, and strained public services. For instance, in cities like Mumbai and São Paulo, the influx of people has led to severe traffic congestion, pollution, and a lack of basic amenities for many. Moreover, the environmental impact of urban sprawl, including the destruction of natural habitats and increased carbon emissions, is a growing concern. These negative effects can, in turn, diminish the overall quality of life for city inhabitants.


In my opinion, while urban growth can bring short-term benefits, the long-term challenges, such as environmental degradation and social inequality, cannot be ignored. To maximize the advantages of population growth in cities, it is crucial for governments to implement sustainable urban planning, invest in public infrastructure, and ensure that economic benefits are widely distributed.

Question

Some people believe that hard work and determination are the main requirements to become successful in life, while others say other factors lead to success. 


Discuss both these views and give your own opinion.

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

The ongoing discourse surrounding the ingredients for achieving success in life has persisted for decades. Some assert that success hinges primarily on hard work and perseverance, while others argue that factors such as financial means and physical attractiveness wield greater influence. Both viewpoints warrant examination, and the multifaceted nature of success suggests that a combination of these factors is likely to be most effective.

 

On one side of the debate, proponents of diligence and determination assert that sustained exertion and a resilient mindset are fundamental to success. They contend that individuals who possess a strong work ethic and are resolved to surmount obstacles are more apt to persevere in challenging circumstances. Success, from this perspective, is the product of consistent endeavor, learning from setbacks, and the ability to adapt to changing situations.

 

Conversely, advocates for the significance of external factors such as wealth and appearance argue that societal expectations and biases can significantly shape one's opportunities. Financial resources can grant access to superior education, professional networks, and opportunities that might otherwise be inaccessible. Likewise, physical attractiveness is often associated with confidence and societal preferences, potentially influencing both professional and social outcomes.

 

In my view, while hard work and determination are undoubtedly pivotal components of success, it is unrealistic to disregard entirely the impact of external factors. A balanced perspective recognizes the interaction of these elements, acknowledging that diligence and determination lay the groundwork, while financial means and physical appearance may act as catalysts, unlocking opportunities and facilitating advancement.

 

In conclusion, the debate regarding the determinants of success underscores the intricacy of human accomplishment. Finding equilibrium and harnessing both personal exertion and external advantages can optimize one's prospects for success.

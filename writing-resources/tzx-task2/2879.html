Question

Competitiveness is considered to be an important quality for people in many societies.


How do you think it affects individuals?


Is it a positive or negative trend?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

It is clear that many countries around the world perceive competitiveness as essential to pursuing one's goals and achieving success. There are a  number of ways competiveness affects individuals, and I believe that it is a mostly negative trend. 


 Competitiveness can affect people in various ways. At the individual level, competitiveness may compel people to set goals for themselves and have higher ambitions. This allows them to develop crucial skills such as  determination, perseverance, and resilience and pushes them to always try their best. Furthermore, competitive activities can also urge people to challenge the status quo and expand their comfort zone, which can enable them to be more adaptive, effective problem-solvers. By taking risks and overcoming obstacles, people are more likely to discover and pursue new opportunities for themselves, whether in their professional careers or  academic lives. 


 However, I believe that the negative repercussions of competitiveness outweigh the benefits. First of all, competitiveness can drastically lower people's self-esteem and instil a lot of self-doubt in individuals. It is inevitable that there are people who will always be more capable and successful, perhaps due to their inherent skillsets or privileged backgrounds. Thus, when people compare themselves to others, they are likely to feel frustrated and inadequate, which would negatively impact their mental health. On top of that, competitiveness can also breed anger, distrust, and hostility among individuals. People who are highly competitive tend to view others as competitors who must be defeated—an unhealthy attitude that discourages cooperation and empathy. When people can only think about elevating themselves, they are less likely to be concerned about others, which is detrimental to social harmony.   


 In conclusion, competitiveness affects people in many ways, and I contend that the demerits ultimately outweigh the merits.

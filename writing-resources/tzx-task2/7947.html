Question

Many working people do not have enough free time off work to spend with their families and friends. 


Why? 


What are the effects on family life and society?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In contemporary society, many working people do not have enough free time to spend with their families and friends. This phenomenon is driven by specific social and professional factors, and it brings negative effects on both family life and society as a whole.


There are two main reasons why working individuals struggle to find time for their loved ones. First, the competitive job market pushes people to work longer hours to secure their positions or pursue career advancement. Many employees, especially in fields like technology or finance, feel pressured to take on extra tasks or work overtime to meet high performance standards—fearful that falling behind could lead to job loss. For example, staff in the tech industry often spend long hours fixing software problems or developing new programs to keep up with rapid technological changes. Second, the desire for higher income also plays a role. With rising living costs (such as housing, education, and healthcare expenses), many people choose to work more, or even take on second jobs, to improve their family’s financial situation. They prioritize earning more money over leisure time, believing this will provide better security for their families.


The lack of free time for family and friends has harmful impacts on family life first. When people spend most of their time at work, they miss out on important family moments—like their children’s school events or family gatherings. This can weaken emotional bonds between family members: children may feel neglected by parents who are rarely present, and couples may grow apart due to lack of communication. Over time, this can lead to family conflicts or even breakdowns. For instance, some parents who work overtime every day often fail to have regular conversations with their kids, making it hard to understand their children’s needs and feelings.


However，this trend also affects society negatively. If many families face relationship problems, it can increase social instability. For example, children from families with little parental companionship may be more likely to feel lonely or develop behavioral issues, which could lead to problems like dropping out of school or getting involved in crime later. Additionally, when people work too much without rest, they are more likely to suffer from health problems like stress or fatigue. This not only reduces their quality of life but also puts a strain on public healthcare systems, as more people need medical treatment for work-related illnesses.


In conclusion, the lack of free time among working people is mainly caused by job competition and the need for higher income. It harms family relationships by weakening emotional connections and creates problems for society such as increased instability and pressure on healthcare.

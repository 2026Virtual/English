Question

Some people think the increasing business and cultural contact between countries bring many positive effects. Others, however, feel that this will lead to the loss of national identities. 


Discuss both views and give your opinion.

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

Nowadays, international business and cultural exchange are becoming more common due to globalization. While some people believe that these connections bring many benefits to individuals and societies, others are concerned that they may cause the disappearance of unique national identities. In my opinion, although there are risks, the positive impacts of closer global contact are greater.

 

On the one hand, those who worry about globalization often point to the loss of cultural traditions. When people are constantly exposed to foreign movies, fashion, and food, they may gradually lose interest in their own customs. For example, in many Asian countries, young people now prefer celebrating Western festivals such as Christmas rather than traditional ones. Similarly, the dominance of international companies, such as fast-food chains or clothing brands, can reduce the visibility of local businesses. Over time, this may weaken national pride and make different countries look increasingly similar.

 

On the other hand, stronger business and cultural ties can bring clear advantages. First, international trade creates more jobs and improves economic growth, which directly raises people’s living standards. Second, cultural contact allows individuals to learn from one another. For instance, students who study abroad not only gain knowledge but also become more open-minded and tolerant. Furthermore, cultural exchange does not necessarily mean losing identity. In fact, it can encourage people to value their own traditions more, as they are proud to share them with others on the global stage.

 

In conclusion, although globalization may pose a threat to certain cultural traditions, I believe the overall benefits, such as economic development and mutual understanding, far outweigh the disadvantages. Instead of resisting foreign influence, countries should find a balance by protecting their heritage while embracing international cooperation.

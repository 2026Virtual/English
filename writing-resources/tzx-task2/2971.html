Question

In many countries, traditional foods are being replaced by international fast foods. This is having a negative effect on both families and societies.


To what extent do you agree or disagree with this opinion?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

It is true that international fast food has been slowly but surely replacing traditional cuisine in numerous countries. Although there are some benefits to this trend, I believe that it is ultimately harmful to people and society. 


On one hand, the growing popularity of fast food presents people with more eating options. It can be tiring, after all, to eat the same kind of food day in and day out. Having a greater variety of foods means people get to enjoy their meals more, as sometimes they might be in the mood for, say, a  burger instead of rice and vegetables. On top of that, fast food is arguably more flavourful and delicious than most traditional meals beca use of the large amounts of added salt, sugar or butter. They're also often fried, which gives a crispy and satisfying texture to the bite.


Despite these advantages to fast food, I would still argue that their replacement of  traditional foods is a negative development. First, the same ingredients and cooking methods that give fast food their delicious flavour also make them thoroughly unhealthy, causing heart disease, obesity and even cancer. As a result, nations which consume a lot of fast food tend to have lower life expectancy, as is the case with the United States. Moreover, a decline in traditional foods contributes to the decline of traditional culture. We should try to prevent that, for in this day and age of globalisation, every bit of cultural preservation counts. 


In summary, there are a few upsides to the overtaking of traditional cuisine by fast food, but it is mostly detrimental to people and society.

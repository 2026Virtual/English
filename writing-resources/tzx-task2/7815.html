Question

In the past, people stored knowledge in books. Nowadays people store knowledge on the Internet.


What are the advantages and disadvantages of this trend?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In recent years, the way people access and store knowledge has changed significantly. While books used to be the primary source of information in the past, the Internet has now taken a leading role. This trend brings a number of advantages, but also some disadvantages that should not be ignored.


One of the main advantages of storing knowledge on the Internet is convenience. People can find information on almost any topic within seconds, which is especially useful for students, professionals, and researchers. For example, instead of spending hours searching through books in a library, one can simply type keywords into a search engine and get instant results. Another benefit is accessibility. Unlike physical books that are limited by location and availability, online knowledge can be accessed from anywhere at any time, as long as there is an Internet connection.


However, there are also some drawbacks to this shift. Firstly, the reliability of information on the Internet can be a concern. Not all sources are accurate or trustworthy, and people may struggle to tell the difference between facts and misinformation. Secondly, dependence on digital tools may lead to a decline in deep reading habits. Many people now skim web pages instead of reading in depth, which may affect their ability to understand and retain complex ideas. Finally, storing everything online also raises concerns about privacy and data security, especially when sensitive knowledge or research is involved.


In conclusion, while storing knowledge on the Internet offers greater convenience and access, it also presents challenges related to reliability, reading quality, and data protection. In my view, the best approach is to use both digital and traditional sources wisely, depending on the context and purpose of the information needed.

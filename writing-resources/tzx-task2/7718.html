Question

In many countries today, people in cities either live alone or in small family units, rather than in large, extended family groups. 


Is this a positive or negative trend?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In recent years, the trend of living alone or in small family units has become increasingly common in urban areas, replacing the traditional extended family structure. While some argue that this shift offers more privacy and personal freedom, I believe it has more negative consequences for individuals and society as a whole.


One major drawback is the decline in emotional and practical support. In extended families, grandparents, aunts, and uncles often help with childcare, household tasks, and financial difficulties. For example, when parents are busy with work, relatives can step in to care for children, reducing stress and strengthening family bonds. In contrast, small families or individuals living alone must handle everything themselves, leading to higher levels of loneliness and stress. Additionally, older family members, who traditionally rely on younger generations for care, may suffer from neglect when families become smaller and more scattered, increasing the risk of social isolation.


Supporters of smaller households claim that they provide greater personal freedom and reduce family conflicts. While it is true that living alone allows for more privacy, the long-term effects—such as weaker family bonds and increased social isolation—outweigh these benefits. Governments and communities should encourage stronger family connections by providing housing policies that accommodate multi-generational living and organizing community events to foster interaction.


In conclusion, although living in smaller family units may seem convenient, the loss of emotional support and social connections makes this trend largely negative. Society should take steps to preserve the benefits of extended family structures while adapting to modern lifestyles.

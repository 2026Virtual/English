Question

Some people think all lawbreakers should be sent to prison, while others think there are better alternatives for those whose crimes are minor, such as making them do work for the local community. 


Discuss both these views and give your own opinion.


Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

It is widely debated whether all lawbreakers should be punished by imprisonment or if some minor offenders should be given alternative penalties, such as community service. While putting every criminal in jail can seem like a clear and consistent way to maintain law and order, I believe that for small crimes, non-custodial sentences are often more beneficial to both the offender and society.


On the one hand, some people argue that all lawbreakers should be treated equally and sent to prison. They believe that this sends a strong message that breaking the law will not be tolerated under any circumstances. Imprisonment is also seen as an effective way to keep society safe, as criminals are kept away from the public. For example, if even petty theft is punished by jail time, others might think twice before committing a similar crime. In this way, strict enforcement could act as a strong deterrent.


However, others believe that minor crimes do not always require such harsh punishment. Sending people to prison for small offenses may overcrowd prisons and waste public resources. Also, for first-time or non-violent offenders, prison can be a harmful experience, making them more likely to reoffend after release. Instead, alternatives like community service can help these individuals repay their debt to society in a positive way. For instance, a person who damages public property could be ordered to clean parks or streets, which is both meaningful and corrective.


In my opinion, while prison is necessary for serious crimes, it is not always the best solution for every case. We should consider the nature of the crime, the intent of the offender, and whether a more constructive punishment might lead to better results. Letting minor offenders contribute to the community could help them learn responsibility and avoid future mistakes.


In conclusion, although imprisonment is sometimes necessary to ensure justice and public safety, it is important to use more flexible alternatives for minor crimes. This can lead to more effective rehabilitation and reduce the pressure on the prison system.

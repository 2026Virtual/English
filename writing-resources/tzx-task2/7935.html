Question

Some people think it is better to study in a large class size whereas some others argue that it is better to study in a class with a small number of students. 


Discuss both sides and give your opinion

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

The size of the class can significantly impact the effectiveness of learning. There are differing views on whether it is better to study in a large class or a small one. While some argue that larger classrooms offer more opportunities for diverse interactions, I believe that smaller classes provide distinct advantages in terms of focus and personalized attention.


On the one hand, proponents of larger classes argue that learning with a greater number of peers offers several benefits. First, they contend that a larger class exposes students to a variety of perspectives, which can enrich their understanding of topics and help them gain knowledge from individuals with different backgrounds. Additionally, interacting with a diverse group of students can foster tolerance and provide opportunities to learn about different cultures. This experience, they argue, better prepares students for working in multicultural environments and adapting to a globalized world.


On the other hand, I believe that smaller classes offer more significant advantages. First, having fewer students in a class leads to a more focused and less distracting learning environment. This allows students to pay closer attention to the lecturer and better absorb the material being taught. Second, small class sizes benefit teachers as well. With fewer students to manage, teachers can more easily assess each individual’s strengths and weaknesses, tailoring their instruction to meet specific needs. This personalized approach fosters a deeper understanding and more effective learning for all students.


In conclusion, while large classes offer opportunities for students to learn from a broader range of peers and develop cultural awareness, I firmly believe that smaller classes provide more focused and customized learning experiences. Thus, I believe that the advantages of smaller classes outweigh those of larger ones in terms of educational outcomes.

Question

Some people think that the increasing use of computers and mobile phones for communication has a negative effect on young people’s reading and writing skills.


To what extent do you agree or disagree?  

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

The growing reliance on computers and mobile phones for communication has sparked a debate about its impact on the reading and writing skills of young people. Some argue that these technological tools negatively affect literacy, while others believe they offer unique advantages. I believe that both perspectives have merit.


On the positive side, computers and mobile phones have revolutionized the way young people access information and communicate. The Internet provides a vast array of resources that can enhance reading comprehension and expose individuals to diverse writing styles. Online platforms, blogs, and social media offer opportunities for young people to engage in written expression, sharing their thoughts and ideas with a global audience. This exposure can contribute to the development of digital literacy and provide a platform for creative writing. Moreover, the interactive nature of digital content, such as e-books with multimedia elements, can make reading more engaging and accessible for young people.


However, it is crucial to acknowledge the potential drawbacks. The informal nature of digital communication often involves abbreviations, acronyms, and emoticons, which may contribute to a decline in formal writing skills. The ease of autocorrect and predictive text features in digital devices can lead to a decline in spelling and grammar proficiency. Young people may become reliant on these tools, potentially diminishing their attention to detail in written communication. Additionally, the constant exposure to short, rapid-fire content on social media may affect attention spans, potentially impacting deep reading habits. The decline in long-form reading can hinder the ability to comprehend and analyze complex texts, a skill crucial for academic and professional success.


In conclusion, the impact of computers and mobile phones on the reading and writing skills of young people is nuanced. While these technologies offer valuable opportunities for digital literacy and creative expression, they also pose challenges to formal writing conventions and traditional reading skills. The key lies in striking a balance - leveraging the benefits of technology while also emphasizing the importance of traditional literacy skills to ensure that young people are equipped with a well-rounded set of communication abilities.

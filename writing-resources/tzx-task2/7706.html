Question

Some schoolchildren dislike maths. 


What are the reasons? 


What are the best ways to help these children enjoy learning maths?


Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

Mathematics is a fundamental subject that plays a crucial role in everyday life and various professional fields. However, a significant number of schoolchildren express a dislike for maths, finding it either too difficult or uninteresting. This essay will explore the main reasons behind this aversion and suggest effective ways to make learning maths more enjoyable.

 

One primary reason why children dislike maths is its abstract nature. Unlike subjects such as history or literature, which involve storytelling and real-life scenarios, maths consists of numbers, formulas, and logical structures that can seem disconnected from daily experiences. This makes it difficult for some students to grasp concepts and see the practical relevance of what they are learning. Additionally, many children develop a fear of failure due to the subject’s right-or-wrong nature. Unlike essay-based subjects where creativity is valued, maths often requires precise answers, making students feel discouraged when they struggle to solve problems correctly.

 

To help children enjoy learning maths, teachers and parents can adopt a more engaging and practical approach. One effective method is incorporating real-life applications, such as using maths in shopping, cooking, or budgeting, to show its relevance. Moreover, interactive activities like games, puzzles, and digital apps can make the learning process more enjoyable and less stressful. Teachers should also encourage a growth mindset by praising effort rather than just correct answers, helping students build confidence and resilience in problem-solving.

 

In conclusion, children’s dislike for maths often stems from its abstract nature and the pressure to achieve correct answers. By making the subject more engaging through real-world applications and interactive methods, educators can help students develop a more positive attitude towards maths and improve their overall learning experience.

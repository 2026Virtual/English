Question

Some people say it is acceptable to use animals for our benefit, others say it is wrong to exploit them. 


Discuss both points of view and give your opinion.


Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

The exploitation of animals by humans has become a widely debated issue. Some people argue that the cruelty humans inflict on animals is unacceptable, while others believe animals should be used for food and medical testing. Although there are strong arguments on both sides, I align myself with the latter group for several reasons.

 

On the one hand, animal rights activists argue that animals should have the same rights as humans due to ethical considerations and concerns for animal welfare. Specifically, animals, like humans, have the capacity to feel pain, suffer, and experience emotions. Furthermore, according to animal liberation philosophy, all living creatures have a right to life. Therefore, it is unethical to slaughter or poach animals for their meat, skin, or other valuable organs, as this also leads to habitat destruction. As a result, animal rights advocates claim that animals should not be used to satisfy human needs.

 

On the other hand, supporters of animal exploitation argue that it is a necessity for practical reasons, such as survival and scientific advancement. They point out that the use of animals for food, clothing, and transportation has been a part of human society for centuries, and eliminating this practice would be impractical. In addition, animals play a crucial role in medical and scientific advancements, particularly in testing the safety of products and treatments. For example, during the COVID-19 pandemic, scientists conducted experiments on animals to test the safety and effectiveness of vaccines, which were later used to treat humans. While this may seem cruel, many argue that the use of animals is vital for ensuring human health and safety.

 

In conclusion, while animal rights activists present valid ethical concerns about the exploitation of animals, I believe that the use of animals for food, medical testing, and other practical purposes is a necessity for human survival and scientific progress.

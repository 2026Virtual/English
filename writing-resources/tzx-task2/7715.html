Question

It is more important to spend public money on promoting a healthy lifestyle in order to prevent illness than to spend it on treatment of people who are already ill. 


To what extent do you agree or disagree?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In recent years, the allocation of public funds to healthcare has sparked considerable debate. Some argue it is more essential to invest in promoting healthy lifestyles to prevent diseases rather than treating individuals who have already fallen ill. Personally, I agree that prioritizing preventive measures offers greater benefits in the long run.


Firstly, investing in preventive healthcare measures encourages individuals to adopt healthier living habits, significantly reducing the risk of common illnesses. Educational campaigns, fitness programs, and nutrition awareness initiatives can effectively decrease the occurrence of lifestyle-related diseases such as obesity, diabetes, and heart conditions. For instance, countries like Japan have demonstrated that continuous investment in public health education leads to a notably healthier population, consequently relieving pressure on the healthcare system and saving considerable costs in the long term.


However, opponents argue that allocating funds primarily for prevention may leave insufficient resources to support people currently suffering from illnesses. Admittedly, treatment for existing conditions is vital and cannot be neglected, especially for chronic or severe cases requiring urgent care. Yet, it is essential to recognize that prioritizing preventive health initiatives can significantly lower the incidence rate of diseases, thus reducing the overall demand for medical treatments. Striking a balanced approach, with greater emphasis on preventive healthcare, ensures sustainable public health management while still providing essential support for immediate medical needs.


In conclusion, despite the undeniable necessity of treating existing illnesses, prioritizing preventive healthcare through promoting healthy lifestyles proves to be a more effective long-term strategy. Such an approach not only improves the general well-being of the population but also ensures more efficient use of funds.

Question

Some people believe that a lot of scientific research done today is a waste of time and money. 


To what extent do you agree or disagree?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In the modern world, scientific research has become increasingly significant, influencing nearly every aspect of human life. I strongly believe that it is worth investing substantial amounts of time and money, as the benefits of scientific research are undeniable.


One major area where scientific inquiry has proved indispensable is healthcare. Ongoing research drives the development of innovative treatments and life-saving medicines. For example, during the COVID-19 pandemic, scientists worked intensively to develop vaccines that ultimately contained the spread of the virus and saved millions of lives worldwide. Without such breakthroughs, modern healthcare would struggle to address urgent health crises.


Another compelling reason is the advancement of technology. Scientific research has been the foundation for inventions that shape our daily lives. For instance, the internet, which initially emerged as a scientific project, has now become the backbone of the global economy. Moreover, digital devices such as smartphones, laptops, and computers, which stem from scientific progress, have transformed the way people learn, work, and communicate.


Furthermore, research plays a crucial role in tackling global challenges such as climate change, environmental degradation, and energy shortages. For example, scientists have developed renewable energy sources—including solar, wind, and nuclear power—as viable alternatives to fossil fuels, which severely damage the environment. The adoption of these cleaner technologies not only alleviates the pressure on ecosystems but also promotes sustainable development worldwide.


In conclusion, far from being a waste of time or money, scientific research is essential to the progress of modern society. Its contributions to medicine, technology, and global problem-solving demonstrate that continued investment in research is fundamental for building a healthier, more sustainable, and more prosperous future.

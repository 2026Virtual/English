Question

Nowadays, not enough students choose science subjects in universities in many countries.


Why is this? 


What are the effects on society? 

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In today’s knowledge-based society, science and technology are advancing at an unprecedented rate. Despite this rapid progress, the numbers of university students who choose science subjects show a declining trend in many countries. This essay will analyze its underlying reasons and negative effects on society.

The first reason for this trend may involve the recognition that science subjects are more challenging than other subjects. Obtaining degrees in science requires more effort. For instance, to graduate from the school of physics or chemistry, students have to learn a wide range of theoretical courses and spend many years working in laboratories. This process is lengthy and tedious, which may dissuade many young people from pursuing science as a major.

Moreover, a lack of interest in science contributes to the low enrollment in science subjects. In a society where fame and fortune determine a person’s value, younger generations tend to idolize popular stars and influencers rather than scientists. Additionally, primary and secondary school curricula often fail to adequately emphasize the importance and appeal of science. With limited knowledge and little interest in science, fewer people choose science subjects when they enter university.

The declining number of students in science subjects at universities has profound effects on society. There will be a shortage of personnel capable of conducting scientific study and research, as universities reduce the supply of qualified graduates majoring in science. Declining scientific research could lead to hindered innovation and technological stagnation, negatively affecting economic growth and social progress. Countries with fewer skilled scientists may struggle to keep up with advancements made by others, thus losing competitiveness in the international arena.

In conclusion, the declining enrollment in science subjects is attributed to multiple factors, and this issue can have serious consequences for society. Efforts should be made at all levels of education to foster interest in science and to emphasize its importance in driving progress and innovation. Only by addressing these challenges can we ensure a brighter future for science and society as a whole.

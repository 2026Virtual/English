Question

Many employees can now do their work from home using modern technology. However, this change may only benefit workers, not the employers.


To what extent do you agree or disagree? 

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

Traditionally, work was confined to specific locations such as offices or factories. However, with advancements in modern technology, an increasing number of people are now working from home via the internet. I believe this new mode of work benefits both employees and employers.


It is undeniable that remote work offers numerous advantages for employees. Firstly, employees do not need to commute between home and the office, saving a significant amount of time and energy. This is particularly beneficial for those living in large cities, where traffic congestion or crowded subways make commuting a daily ordeal. Another benefit of working from home is the ability to create a work schedule that aligns with personal life, leading to a better work-life balance. This flexibility can reduce stress and increase job satisfaction, potentially resulting in higher productivity.


From the perspective of employers, allowing employees to work from home is also advantageous. One of the primary benefits is the reduction in overhead costs. Companies can save substantial amounts on electricity bills, office equipment, and furniture, not to mention the exorbitant office rent in business districts. In addition to cost savings, remote work enables companies to gain flexibility in their recruitment and assessment processes. Employers can hire the best candidates from a global talent pool, regardless of geographical location. They can also shift from traditional assessment mechanisms to ones that focus on the results of employees’ work instead of the amount of time they spend in the workplace.


Furthermore, remote work can lead to increased employee retention. When employees have the flexibility to work from home, they are more likely to stay with the company long-term. This reduces turnover rates and the associated costs of hiring and training new staff. Additionally, a remote work policy can enhance a company’s reputation as a modern and flexible employer, making it more attractive to top talent.


In conclusion, working remotely is beneficial to both employees and employers, offering convenience, flexibility, and cost efficiency. The advantages for employees include saving time and achieving a better work-life balance, while employers benefit from reduced overhead costs, access to a global talent pool, and improved employee retention.

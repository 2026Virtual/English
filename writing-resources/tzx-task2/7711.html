Question

Some people think that the main benefit of international cooperation is in the protection of the environment, while others believe that businesses mainly benefit. 


Discuss both these views and give your own opinion.

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

The importance of international cooperation is widely recognized, but opinions differ on its primary benefits. While some argue that its greatest value lies in environmental protection, others contend that businesses reap the most advantages. In my view, both perspectives hold merit, as global collaboration serves dual purposes—safeguarding the planet while fostering economic growth.


On one hand, cross-border cooperation is essential for addressing environmental challenges. Climate change, deforestation, and pollution do not respect national boundaries, making joint efforts crucial. International agreements like the Paris Accord demonstrate how countries can unite to set emission targets and share sustainable technologies. For instance, wealthier nations often fund renewable energy projects in developing countries, reducing global carbon footprints collectively. Without such collaboration, individual countries' efforts would be far less effective in combating planetary crises.


On the other hand, businesses undeniably benefit from international partnerships. Globalization allows companies to access new markets, optimize supply chains, and share innovations. Multinational corporations, such as electric vehicle manufacturers, thrive by leveraging international expertise and resources. Trade agreements reduce tariffs, enabling cost-efficient production and higher profitability. However, critics argue that this economic focus sometimes overshadows ecological responsibilities, as seen in cases where corporations exploit weaker environmental regulations in certain countries.

Ultimately, the most balanced approach integrates both priorities. Environmental protection ensures long-term planetary health, which is fundamental for stable economies, while business growth drives the innovation and funding needed for green initiatives. Therefore, international cooperation should aim for synergy between ecological and economic gains, rather than treating them as competing interests.

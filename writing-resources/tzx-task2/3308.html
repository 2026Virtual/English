Question

People continue to commit crimes even after being punished for it.


Why do you think this happens?


How can crime be stopped?


Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

It is true that many offenders continue to commit crimes even after being imprisoned for their wrongdoing. There are several contributing factors for recidivism among criminals and a number of possible solutions that can be implemented to decrease the likelihood of repeat offenders.


The causes of crimes are complex and manifold. However, the most significant risk factors include poverty, one’s inability to obtain employment, and mental health issues aggravated by a brutal and inhumane criminal justice system. Prisoners who are released often have a considerably difficult time reintegrating into society. Not only are they traumatised by the harsh conditions in prison, they are also permanently labelled as a “criminal” and alienated by other members of society, including their family and friends. Furthermore, having a criminal record  makes it significantly more difficult to obtain a job, so the employment options for former prisoners are severely limited. This combination of social stigma and lack of employment options can be devastating for former prisoners, leading them back into a life of crime.


Several possible measures can be taken to prevent recidivism. First of all, prisons need to shift their focus from punishment to rehabilitation. All too often, incarceration facilities subject prisoners to unthinkable levels of abuse, violence and deprivation. Mistreatment of inmates causes them to have greater levels of anger and anguish, which may lead them to become mentally unstable and seek revenge on society in the future. In addition, prison-based education programs and job training should be implemented to help prisoners develop the occupational skills they’ll need for life after prison. Finally, substance abuse and mental health issues, which are common among prisoners, need to be properly diagnosed and treated to help prisoners reintegrate into society.  


In conclusion, it is clear that many criminals continue to commit crimes despite being punished. However, several measures can be taken to effectively combat this issue.

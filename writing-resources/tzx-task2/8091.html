Question

 In many countries, young people increasingly spend their free time in shopping centres instead of engaging in traditional leisure activities such as sports or music. 


Do you think this is a positive or negative development?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In recent years, it has become common for young people to spend much of their free time in shopping centres rather than doing traditional leisure activities like sports or music. In my view, this is a negative development overall, even though it may bring some short-term benefits.


On the one hand, there are a few reasons why spending time in shopping centres might be attractive to young people. Firstly, malls are safe, comfortable and provide a wide range of entertainment options, from restaurants to cinemas. Young people may find them convenient places to meet friends and relax. Secondly, shopping centres also reflect modern consumer culture, where buying fashionable clothes or electronic devices can give young people a sense of identity and belonging. In this sense, visiting malls may play a role in their social lives.


On the other hand, I would argue that the disadvantages of this trend are more significant. The most obvious problem is that young people may neglect healthier activities such as sports. If they spend hours walking around malls instead of playing football, swimming or exercising, this could harm their physical fitness in the long term. Another concern is that focusing on consumerism may discourage them from developing meaningful skills. For example, learning to play a musical instrument or practising a team sport not only brings enjoyment but also builds discipline, teamwork and creativity. By contrast, shopping mainly promotes materialism and short-term pleasure. In addition, there are financial consequences. Regular visits to shopping centres often involve spending money on unnecessary items, which may create financial pressure for families and encourage wasteful habits. Over time, this could lead to a consumer-driven lifestyle that is difficult to change.


In conclusion, while shopping centres may provide convenience and social opportunities for young people, I believe the overall impact of spending too much free time there is negative. It takes away time from healthier and more rewarding activities such as sports or music, and it encourages materialism rather than personal growth.

Question

It is becoming very common these days for students to take courses over the Internet instead of in face-to-face classrooms.


Why are more students choosing this way of learning?


Is this a positive or a negative development?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In recent years, online education has grown significantly, with many students opting for digital courses over traditional classroom learning. This trend is driven by several factors, and while it offers notable benefits, it also presents certain drawbacks.


One major reason for the popularity of online learning is its flexibility. Students can access course materials anytime and anywhere, making it ideal for those balancing work, family, or other commitments. For example, working professionals can upskill without quitting their jobs, and parents can study while managing childcare. Additionally, online courses often cost less than traditional education, as they eliminate expenses like commuting and campus fees. Platforms like Coursera and Khan Academy also provide high-quality education from top institutions, making learning more accessible globally.


However, this shift also has downsides. A lack of face-to-face interaction can reduce student engagement and make it harder to build relationships with peers and instructors. Some learners struggle with self-discipline in an unstructured environment, leading to lower completion rates. Furthermore, not all subjects—such as lab-based sciences or hands-on training—can be effectively taught online. Without proper supervision, academic integrity issues like cheating may also increase.


In conclusion, while online learning offers convenience and affordability, it cannot fully replace traditional classrooms. A balanced approach, combining digital and in-person education, may be the best way forward to ensure effective and inclusive learning for all.

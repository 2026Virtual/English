Question

The availability of entertainment such as video games on handheld devices is harmful to individuals and to the society they live in. 


To what extent do you agree and disagree?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In recent years, the issue of entertainment on handheld devices, such as video games, has sparked considerable debate in society. While I acknowledge that this trend may have some negative effects, I firmly believe that it is not entirely harmful, primarily due to the development of thinking skills and the improvement of online social interaction.

 

On the one hand, it is reasonable to argue that mobile video games can cause problems for several reasons. The first reason is health concerns. To elaborate, spending too much time playing games can lead to less physical activity and poor health. For instance, research from the World Health Organization indicates that teenagers who play mobile games for long hours are more likely to suffer from obesity and back pain. Moreover, a decline in face-to-face communication also supports this view. In particular, many young people may become addicted to games and avoid real-life social interactions. As a result, they may struggle with social skills in daily life.

 

On the other hand, I would contend that video games on handheld devices also offer positive outcomes. Firstly, they help develop problem-solving abilities. To clarify, many games require planning and decision-making, which improve thinking. A good example is puzzle games, which have been shown to improve memory and attention. Secondly, they promote social connection. That is, online multiplayer games allow people to talk, cooperate, and make friends. By contrast, people without these experiences may feel more isolated.

 

In conclusion, although I understand the rationale behind the concern that video games may cause health and social problems, I remain convinced that they provide useful mental and social benefits, making them more helpful than harmful.

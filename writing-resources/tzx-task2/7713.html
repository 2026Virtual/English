Question

Some people believe that charity organisations should give aid to those in greatest need, wherever they live. Others believe that charity organisations should focus on assisting people in their own country instead. 


Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In today's interconnected world, charitable organizations face a difficult choice: should they prioritize helping those in extreme poverty worldwide, or concentrate on supporting disadvantaged groups within their own countries? While both approaches have merit, I believe that while local charities play a crucial role, international aid should not be neglected.


Those who argue for focusing on domestic aid often highlight practical and ethical reasons. Firstly, it is easier to assess needs and monitor the impact of donations within one's own country due to familiarity with local issues and systems. For example, food banks and homeless shelters can directly address poverty in their communities with immediate effect. Secondly, taxpayers and donors may prefer their contributions to benefit fellow citizens, especially during economic crises when local unemployment or homelessness rises. National loyalty and cultural ties also make domestic charity more politically acceptable in some cases.


However, limiting aid to national borders ignores the extreme suffering in developing nations. In many war-torn regions or drought-affected areas, international aid is the only lifeline for survival. Organizations like UNICEF and Oxfam provide vaccines, clean water, and emergency food supplies that save millions of lives annually—support that local governments in poor countries cannot offer. Moreover, global problems like pandemics or climate change require cross-border cooperation. For instance, during COVID-19, wealthier nations' donations of vaccines to poorer countries helped control the virus worldwide.


In my view, a balanced approach is essential. Wealthier nations should primarily address domestic poverty but also allocate a portion of resources to global emergencies. Individuals can contribute to both local food drives and international disaster relief funds. After all, humanity's greatest challenges demand both national responsibility and global solidarity.

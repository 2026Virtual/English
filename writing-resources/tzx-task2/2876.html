Question

Some people think that detailed crime descriptions on newspapers and TV have bad consequences, so this kind of information should be restricted on media.


To what extent do you agree or disagree with this opinion?


Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

People vary in their opinions regarding the censorship of detailed descriptions of crime. Some are entirely against these descriptions, which they believe lead to harmful repercussions. Although I understand their point of view, I also think there's more value in having these descriptions. 


On one hand, heavy exposure to details of crimes can lower the morale of the general population. It would mislead people into believing that the world is a violent place, when in fact it has never been more peaceful. The truth is most people will never become a victim of crime, so it is pointless to spread fear and paranoia. In addition, revealing the details of crimes may give others the knowledge to execute similar crimes. Children are especially vulnerable to this, as they are more easily influenced and tempted into doings things without considering the consequences. 


Despite these factors, I still believe that it's important to show detailed crime reports. First of all,  law-abiding citizens could use the information to protect themselves. For example, if they know that robberies tend to happen at certain locations after midnight, they could more easily avoid being robbed. As for children, it's important for them to understand that the world isn't completely safe. Too many children are ignorant of potential risks and dangers, so descriptions of crimes help remind them that they should be cautious. With children, it is better to be safe than sorry, even if they are misled into thinking that the world is more dangerous than it actually is. Copycat crimes from children can also be prevented so long as parents properly educate and look after their children. 


In conclusion, I believe that detailed descriptions of crimes provide important benefits that outweigh the negative consequences.

驾照是否要每年一考

观点类
ID: 2973
命中: 2024-12-21
Question

Some people think that the best way to improve road transport safety is to require drivers to retake the test each year.


To what extent do you agree or disagree with this opinion?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

People have different views on whether or not drivers should be required to retake the driving test every year. While I recognise that this requirement would improve road safety somewhat, I believe that ultimately it should not be implemented. 


On one hand, the policy does make some sense, as poor driving is a major reason for car accidents. Every day, millions of car accidents occur, many of them causing serious or fatal injuries. A large portion of these accidents are caused by poor drivers who have either forgotten traffic rules or have developed bad habits over time. Older drivers are another major culprit of deadly accidents  because they often miss traffic lights and signs at busy intersections. Forcing these drivers to pass a yearly driving test would improve road safety, for it would either remind them of traffic rules or prevent them from driving altogether.


However, things are not really that simple. In order to take the driving test, people need to travel to a test location, wait for their turn to take the test, and then take the test. Spending all this time usually forces them to take an entire day off work. Having a mandatory yearly test would therefore cost most adults an annual leave every year, which is no small matter. The driving test is also somewhat expensive, and these costs would add up significantly over the years. It would be better, instead, to implement stricter traffic rules, increase traffic fines, and construct safer roads. 


In conclusion, a mandatory yearly driving test would indeed improve road safety, but I believe this can be accomplished through more sensible and less costly measures.

Question

Large companies should pay higher salaries to CEOs and executives compared with other workers.


To what extent do you agree or disagree?


Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In recent years, the debate about whether large companies should pay higher salaries to CEOs and executives compared with other workers has become increasingly controversial. In my opinion, I believe that CEOs and executives should have higher salaries under reasonable limits.


I personally agree that CEOs and executives should be paid higher salaries than other worker. Companies need to have the best people running them in order to be successful, and the compensation offered to executives should reflect this. If a CEO or executive is highly skilled and experienced, they should be compensated accordingly in order to ensure that they remain with the company. In addition, executives often work long hours and have a great deal of responsibility, so it makes sense to pay them a higher salary to compensate them for these demands.


However, I do believe that there should be limits on the salaries of executives. If the gap between executive salaries and those of other workers becomes too large, it can create tension and resentment within the company, as well as in society as a whole. It is also important to remember that companies exist not only to make a profit, but also to contribute to the well-being of society and the economy. If the salaries of executives become excessive, this can detract from the company's ability to fulfill these other responsibilities.


In conclusion, I believe that large companies should pay higher salaries to CEOs and executives compared with other workers, but within reasonable limits. This will help to attract and retain top talent, and to compensate executives for their responsibilities and long hours. At the same time, however, it is important to avoid excessive salaries that can create tension and harm the company and society.

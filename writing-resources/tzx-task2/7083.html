Question

Some people say governments should give health care the first priorities, while some others believe there are more important priorities to spend the taxpayers’money.


Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

The debate over whether government spending should prioritize health care or other areas reflects two distinct perspectives.


Those who advocate for prioritizing health care argue that a healthy population is essential for the overall well-being and productivity of a nation. They contend that without good health, individuals cannot fully contribute to society or the economy. Access to quality health care can prevent diseases, increase life expectancy, and improve the quality of life for all citizens. Furthermore, many see health care as a fundamental human right, one that the government has a responsibility to protect and ensure is accessible to everyone, regardless of their financial situation.


On the other hand, some argue that other areas, such as education, infrastructure, or economic development, should take precedence over health care in government spending. They believe that education is the cornerstone of a prosperous society, as it provides individuals with the knowledge and skills needed to thrive and innovate. Infrastructure, too, is seen as vital, as it supports the economy by enabling trade, communication, and transportation. Proponents of this view argue that focusing on these areas will create a strong foundation for national growth, which in turn could support and sustain a more effective health care system.


In my opinion, the government should prioritize health care when deciding how to allocate tax revenue. Health is the most basic and essential need of any society; without it, other priorities become secondary. A nation with a healthy population is better equipped to achieve success in education, economic growth, and other areas. Investing in health care not only supports the immediate well-being of citizens but also strengthens the workforce and reduces long-term costs associated with untreated illnesses.


Therefore, health care should be the top priority in government spending, as it is the foundation upon which all other societal progress rests.

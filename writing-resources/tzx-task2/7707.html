Question

Some people think that certain old buildings that are historically important in cities or the countryside should be preserved. 


Do you agree or disagree?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

The preservation of old buildings of historical significance has become a topic sparking heated debate. Some hold the view that these structures should be torn down to pave the way for modern‐day developments. Nevertheless, I am firmly convinced that safeguarding these buildings is of utmost importance.


Firstly, historic buildings act as a tangible link to our past. They provide a unique window into the architectural styles and construction methods characteristic of earlier periods. Whether in urban or rural areas, they play a crucial role in shaping a sense of identity and continuity. Take, for example, those centuries‑old buildings. By maintaining them, we not only enrich the local culture but also turn them into appealing tourist attractions, which in turn gives a boost to the local economy. What's more, these buildings are like living textbooks. They function as open‑air museums, allowing future generations to gain knowledge about the traditions and innovations of past times, thus preserving a precious architectural heritage.


Conversely, those opposed to preservation argue that obsolete buildings can obstruct urban progress and come with high restoration costs. Admittedly, restoration projects do demand a significant amount of financial resources and time. However, considering the long‑term gains such as increased community pride, a stronger sense of community identity, and the promotion of cultural tourism, these short‑term expenses seem well worth it. In the end, striking a balance between respecting historical heritage and meeting modern needs can lead to more harmonious and sustainable development.


In conclusion, despite the challenges involved in preserving old buildings, their contributions to protecting our cultural heritage—as well as their educational and economic values—render their preservation not just a preferable option, but a necessary one.

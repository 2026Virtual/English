Question

Countries with long average working hours are economically successful, but they are also likely to suffer from negative social consequences.  


To what extent do you agree or disagree?


Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

It is undeniable that long average working hours can contribute to economic success. However, some individuals argue that there are several negative aspects that can have a social impact. I agree with this viewpoint and believe that engaging in excessive tasks and spending too much time away from family can have a negative effect on individuals.

 

Long average working hours can bring certain advantages to society in various aspects of life. Firstly, they can stimulate economic growth, resulting in higher incomes and a better quality of life for people. For example, in Japan, implementing 12-hour shifts has led to a rapid increase in the country's GDP, and its population has achieved greater prosperity, alleviating concerns about poverty. Secondly, such measures can enhance a country's power and reputation on the international stage.

 

However, there are significant social drawbacks associated with long working hours. Firstly, individuals who dedicate a considerable amount of time to their work often struggle to manage their time effectively, leading to limited opportunities to spend with their families. This absence can give rise to numerous family problems, such as a lack of happiness among family members. For instance, children may consistently feel alone due to the absence of their parents. Furthermore, the monotonous lifestyle resulting from long working hours can cause exhaustion and depression, ultimately diminishing productivity and creativity.

 

In conclusion, considering the aforementioned reasons, although it is true that working more may offer certain benefits, such as economic improvement, I align myself with the opposing view and firmly believe that the disadvantages outweigh the advantages.

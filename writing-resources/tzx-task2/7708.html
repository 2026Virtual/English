Question

Nowadays, children spend too much time watching TV and playing computer games. 


Some people believe this has negative effects on children’s mental abilities. 


To what extent do you agree or disagree？


Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In recent years, the amount of time children spend on TV and computer games has increased significantly. While some argue that this trend has no major impact, I firmly believe that excessive screen time can harm children’s cognitive development and mental abilities.


Firstly, prolonged exposure to passive entertainment like TV and repetitive computer games can limit critical thinking and creativity. Unlike reading or interactive play, which encourage imagination and problem-solving, screen-based activities often require little mental effort. For example, studies have shown that children who spend hours watching cartoons or playing simple games tend to have shorter attention spans and weaker analytical skills compared to those engaged in more stimulating activities. Additionally, excessive screen time reduces opportunities for real-world social interaction, which is crucial for developing communication and emotional intelligence.


On the other hand, some claim that certain educational programs and games can enhance learning. While it is true that well-designed content can teach problem-solving or language skills, the key issue is moderation. Most children do not use screens for educational purposes but for mindless entertainment. Furthermore, the addictive nature of games and shows can lead to poor time management, leaving less room for physical exercise, homework, or family conversations. Over time, this imbalance can negatively affect memory, focus, and academic performance.


In conclusion, although screens are an unavoidable part of modern life, uncontrolled usage can impair children’s mental growth. Parents and educators should encourage balanced routines that include offline activities to support healthier cognitive development.

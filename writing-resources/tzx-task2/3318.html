Question

Some people think hosting major international sporting events brings a lot of advantages to a country, while others believe there are more disadvantages.


Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

Hosting major international sporting events, such as the Olympics or the World Cup, is often a source of national pride for many countries. However, there is an ongoing debate over whether hosting such events is beneficial or not. In this essay, we will explore both the advantages and disadvantages of hosting major international sporting events and provide our own opinion.

 

On the one hand, hosting major international sporting events can bring many advantages to a country. Firstly, it can boost the local economy through increased tourism and investment. For example, the 2010 World Cup in South Africa generated $5.7 billion in revenue for the country and created 159,000 jobs. Additionally, hosting such events can lead to significant improvements in infrastructure, such as new transport systems and stadiums, which can benefit the local population long after the event has ended.

 

On the other hand, some people believe that hosting major international sporting events can have more disadvantages than advantages. One of the main arguments against hosting such events is that they can be incredibly expensive to organize. The cost of building new infrastructure and providing security can run into billions of dollars, which can be a significant financial burden for the host country. Additionally, the benefits of hosting such events can often be short-lived, with many of the new facilities being left unused or underused after the event has ended.

 

In my opinion, while there are certainly disadvantages to hosting major international sporting events, the benefits can outweigh them if the event is managed correctly. For example, if the event is well-planned and the infrastructure is carefully designed to meet the needs of the local population, then the long-term benefits can be significant. Additionally, if the event is marketed effectively, it can lead to increased tourism and investment, which can have a positive impact on the local economy.

 

In conclusion, hosting major international sporting events can bring both advantages and disadvantages to a country. While the costs of hosting such events can be high, they can also lead to significant long-term benefits if managed correctly. Ultimately, it is up to each individual country to weigh the pros and cons and decide whether hosting such an event is worth the investment.

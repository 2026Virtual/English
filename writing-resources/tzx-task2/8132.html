Question

In many countries, the government spend a large amount money on the arts. Some people agree with this. However, others think government should spend more on health and education. 


Discuss both sides and give your opinion. 

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

In many countries, governments allocate a substantial portion of their budget to the arts. However, some people argue that health and education should be given greater priority. Personally, I believe that investing more in healthcare and education is essential for the long-term development of a nation.


On the one hand, funding the arts is reasonable to a certain extent. Art represents a crucial part of a country’s cultural identity and heritage, and government support is often needed to preserve and promote traditional art forms. Activities such as music, theater, and painting foster creativity and innovation. They can also attract tourists, which contributes to the national economy. For instance, cities with vibrant art scenes often receive more visitors, benefiting local businesses and improving their reputation internationally. Without financial assistance from the government, many of these cultural traditions might disappear, resulting in the loss of cultural diversity.


On the other hand, there are strong arguments for prioritizing health and education. These sectors are fundamental to people’s quality of life and to a nation’s progress. Firstly, access to healthcare ensures that citizens are healthy and productive, which in turn supports economic growth. Moreover, investing in education equips people with knowledge and skills, helping them escape poverty and contribute meaningfully to society. In many developing countries, limited medical facilities and poorly funded schools remain major challenges. For example, rural areas often face shortages of hospitals and qualified teachers, while students in cities sometimes drop out of higher education due to financial difficulties. Therefore, directing more resources to health and education is vital for the stability and prosperity of any nation.


In conclusion, although the arts play an important role in preserving culture and inspiring creativity, I strongly believe that healthcare and education should take precedence. By allocating more funding to these essential sectors, governments can ensure a healthier, more educated, and ultimately more prosperous population.

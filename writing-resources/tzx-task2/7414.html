Question

People today can shop, work, and communicate with others via the Internet instead of face-to-face communication. 


Is it a positive or negative development?


Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your knowledge and experience.

Write at least 250 words.

Sample Answer

The Internet has drastically transformed daily life, enabling people to shop, work, and communicate without face-to-face interactions. While this shift has brought significant convenience and accessibility, it also raises concerns about the potential erosion of social connections and the impact on mental well-being. This essay argues that while online activities offer considerable advantages, their drawbacks must also be carefully considered.

 

On the positive side, the Internet offers unmatched convenience. People can shop for products from around the world, access remote work opportunities, and stay connected with others across distances. Tools like Zoom and Microsoft Teams facilitate business meetings globally, saving time and cutting travel costs. E-commerce websites such as Amazon have made shopping more efficient, especially for those in remote areas. These benefits not only improve productivity but also foster inclusivity, offering services to a broader range of people.

 

However, an over-reliance on online interactions can have negative social and psychological effects. The lack of face-to-face communication weakens interpersonal connections, as non-verbal cues like body language and tone of voice are often absent in digital exchanges. Studies have shown that people who communicate primarily through digital platforms report feeling lonelier than those who engage in person. Additionally, the rise of online shopping has contributed to the decline of local businesses, which struggle to compete with global e-commerce giants.

 

In conclusion, while the Internet has revolutionized the way we shop, work, and communicate, it is crucial to balance its benefits with the potential social and psychological costs. Striking this balance is key to ensuring that the advantages of online interaction do not overshadow the importance of real-world connections and well-being.

Question

The graph below shows the value of clothing exported from Bangladesh between 1988 and 2012, and the pie chart shows the destination and value of exported clothing in 2012.


Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.


Write at least 150 words.


Sample Answer

The line graph illustrates the value of clothing exported from Bangladesh between the time period of 1988 and 2012 while the pie chart mainly reports the destinations and values of these exports in 2012.


It is evident that the value of clothing exports from Bangladesh experienced significant growth over the 24 years. In 2012, the European Union and the USA were the major importers of Bangladeshi clothing while other countries contributed little.


The line graph demonstrates a dramatic increase in the value of clothing exports over the 24-year period. In 1988, the value was below $2 billion. There was a gradual increase until around 2002, after which the growth accelerated significantly. By 2012, the value of clothing exports had reached approximately $20 billion, more than 10 times that of 1988.


In the second pie chart, three-fifths of the total exports went to the European Union, making it the largest importer with a value of $11.38 billion. The USA was the second largest market, importing clothing worth $4.53 billion, comprising 24% of the exports. Additionally, other destinations including Canada ($0.87 billion), Japan ($0.4 billion), Turkey ($0.36 billion), and various other countries, collectively accounted for 16% of the total exports.

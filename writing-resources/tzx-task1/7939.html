Question

The graph below shows the percentage of households with different kinds of technology in the UK from 1997 to 2001.


Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.


Write at least 150 words. 

Sample Answer

The given line graph illustrates the proportion of households in the UK that had access to various types of technological devices and features over a four-year period starting from 1997.


Overall, among the five categories of technology surveyed, CD players were the most commonly owned device, consistently holding the leading position throughout the period. In addition, all five technologies saw a general upward trend in household adoption.


In 1997, CD players were present in 60% of households, the highest figure among all devices. This was followed by computers and DVD players, which were used by around 40% and 28% of households, respectively. Telephones were found in 20% of homes, more than twice the percentage of internet users at that time.


Over the following years, the percentage of households using CD players increased significantly, reaching a peak of approximately 80% in 2001. A similar trend was seen with telephones, whose usage grew steadily to about 76% by the final year. Moreover, household adoption of computers, the internet, and DVD players also rose notably, climbing to nearly 70%, 60%, and 50%, respectively, by the end of the period.

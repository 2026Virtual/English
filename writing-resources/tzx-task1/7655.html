The table below shows the household composition in a North American country from 1970 and 2003.


Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

Sample Answer

The table presents the changes in household composition in a North American country between 1970 and 2003, categorized into family and non-family households.


In family households, the most notable change was seen in married-couple households. In 1970, they accounted for 61% of all households, but by 2003, this percentage had dropped significantly to 41%. In contrast, households with a father and kid, and a mother and kid showed a slight increase. The former rose from 9% to 10%, and the latter from 10% to 11%. Households with other members sharing also had a minor increase to 14%.


Non-family households witnessed more substantial growth. The proportion of single-person households increased from 4% in 1970 to 13% in 2003. Room-sharing households also grew, from 3% to 11%.


Overall, there was a decline in the prevalence of married-couple households, while single-person and room-sharing households became more common, indicating a shift in the household structure in this North American country over the 33-year period.

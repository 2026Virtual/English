Question

The chart below shows the numbers of three types of visitor to a museum between 1997 and 2012.


Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparison where relevant.


Write at least 150 words. 

Sample Answer

The chart illustrates the number of three different types of visitors to a museum between 1997 and 2012.


Overall, the number of adult visitors and special exhibition visitors showed an upward trend, while the number of visitors under the age of 15 declined steadily over the 15-year period.


In 1997, there were 300,100 adult visitors to the museum, and this figure rose slightly to 302,200 in 2002. Similarly, the number of people visiting special exhibitions increased from 28,000 in 1997 to 29,200 in 2002. In contrast, the number of visitors under 15 dropped slightly from 120,000 to 110,000 over the same period.


By 2007, adult visitor numbers had grown significantly, reaching a peak of 400,500. However, this number declined to 380,000 by 2012. Meanwhile, the number of special exhibition visitors continued to rise steadily, increasing from 37,700 in 2007 to 42,000 in 2012. On the other hand, the number of younger visitors continued to fall, decreasing from 87,000 to 82,300 in the final year.

Question

The graph below provides information on car ownership by UK households between 1971 and 2001. 


Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.


Write at least 150 words.

Sample Answer

The bar chart compares the proportion of households with no car, one car, and two cars in the years 1971, 1981, 1991, and 2001.


Overall, there was a clear shift towards greater car ownership over the 30-year period. The percentage of households without a car declined steadily, while ownership of two cars grew significantly. One-car households remained the most common for most of the period but showed some fluctuation.


The proportion of households without a car experienced a marked decline. In 1971, over half of all households (55%) did not own a vehicle. This figure fell sharply to 45% in 1981 and continued to decline, reaching 35% by 1991. By 2001, the percentage had dropped further to just 30%, reflecting improved access to private transport over time.


Households with one car represented 40% in 1971, rising to 45% in 1981 and peaking at 50% in 1991. However, this figure declined slightly to 40% by 2001, possibly due to a growing number of families owning two vehicles. Indeed, ownership of two cars increased steadily throughout the period—from a modest 5% in 1971 to 10% in 1981, then to 15% in 1991, and finally reaching 30% by 2001, matching the figure for households with no car.

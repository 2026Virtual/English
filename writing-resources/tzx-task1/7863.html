Question

The diagram shows rainwater is collected for the use of drinking water in an Australian town.


Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparison where relevant.


Write at least 250 words. 


Sample Answer

The diagram shows the process of collecting, treating, and supplying rainwater as drinking water in a town in Australia.

 

Overall, the process involves several steps, starting with the collection of rainwater from rooftops. The collected water is then filtered, stored, and treated with chemicals before it is delivered to homes for daily use.

 

To begin with, when it rains, water runs off the rooftops and is collected through a system of gutters. It is then directed into underground pipes that carry the water to a filtration system. At this stage, any large particles or debris are removed from the water.

 

After filtration, the water is stored in a large tank. From there, it is pumped into a treatment facility, where chemicals are added to make the water safe for drinking. In the final step, the clean water is transported through pipes and supplied to households for consumption and other domestic purposes.
